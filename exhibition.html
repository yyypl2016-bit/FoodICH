Exhibition Documentation

Opening Reception: Saturday, 1 March 2025, 7PM

"Our cultural heritage is a process" — 我們的文化遺產是一個不斷演進的過程

Cultural heritage consists of not only physical artifacts, but also transient processes, improvised actions, and expressed imaginations. This exhibition presents a unique exploration of Hong Kong's culinary heritage through the lens of technology and art.

體驗

VR Experience

VR experience with 360 video interviews of the chefs and their cooking processes presented alongside AI generated 3D models of dishes in which users could pick-up and inspect at close range.

Several traditional dining environments were created to share the process of material procurement, food preparation, unexpected alternative options, and familial traditions that make up Cantonese culinary heritage.

The work was presented in the exhibition Future Tense at the Hong Kong Art Centre from 1-15 March 2025.

理念

Curatorial Statement

FUTURE TENSE HONG KONG is an art-technology-driven exploration of the future of the intangible side of Hong Kong's cultural heritage, shaped by social change, climate change, and urban development, as imagined by its residents. This exhibition presents a VR navigation app that immersively maps Hong Kong's cultural heritage, a photography and VR exhibit of food imagery generated by workshop participants using state-of-the-art Stable Diffusion technology, two multimedia artworks by Cyan D'Anjou and Meta Objects Studio that speculate on the visual futures seen in immersive form, and works by Paul Shepherd and Inhwa Yeom that immerses the audience in two techno-culture worlds reflecting our own cultural idiosyncrasies. By amplifying the voices and imaginations of its people, the exhibition fosters a collective dialogue about the preservation and transformation of Hong Kong's heritage in an ever-evolving world.
